## Top 3 Realistic Paths

### 1. Employment / Contract Work (Path 5)

**Why it is first:** It is the fastest path to consistent income with the lowest variance. It does not require building a client pipeline, a product, or an audience. It converts existing capability directly into income through a single relationship. A research analyst or risk analyst role at a Solana-focused fund, lending protocol, or DEX is achievable with the demonstrated methodology. A retained contract at $8,000–$15,000/month is achievable with 2–3 warm introductions to the right fund managers or protocol teams.

**The constraint:** Relationship capital. Cold applications to crypto research roles have low conversion rates. The path requires active outreach to specific people at specific organizations, using the research program as a portfolio.

**Time to first dollar:** Weeks to months with active outreach. This is the shortest timeline of any path.

**Income range:** $80,000–$200,000/year for full-time roles; $96,000–$180,000/year for retained contracts. Consistent, not variable.

**Why it is second:** It is the best long-term path if the sales problem is solved. The falsification methodology is the product. The research program is the portfolio. Strategy audits, token risk reviews, and protocol diligence engagements are real services that real clients will pay for. **Time to first dollar:** 1–3 months with active outreach to specific potential clients (crypto funds, DAOs with treasury management mandates, protocol teams facing token design decisions).

**Income range:** $5,000–$25,000 per engagement; $3,000–$10,000/month for retainer relationships. Highly variable until a stable client base is established.

## Top 3 Bad-Fit / Fantasy Paths

### 1. Simple Principal-Capital Carry (Path 1)

This is not a business. It is a leveraged SOL bet with a small yield cushion. The 5.2–6.0% staking yield is entirely funded by SOL inflation — it is a real-terms transfer from non-stakers to stakers, not genuine economic value creation. In 2025, SOL fell approximately 65% from its peak. A 5.8% staking yield on a 65% price decline produces a net loss of approximately 62%. The yield is irrelevant against the price risk.

The deeper problem is that this path does not leverage any of the demonstrated strengths. It requires capital, not research capability. It scales with capital, not with knowledge. It is the path of least resistance, which is exactly why it should be treated with the most suspicion.

**Do not confuse "earning yield on existing SOL holdings" with "a consistent profit path."** The former is fine. The latter is a category error.

### 2. Infrastructure-Adjacent Opportunities (Path 6)

Validator operation and LP provision both fail on the same dimension: they require either significant capital at risk or significant technical investment, and neither leverages the demonstrated research and falsification capability. Validator operation requires attracting $10M+ in delegated stake to generate meaningful income — this takes years and requires a marketing and trust-building effort that is orthogonal to the research skill set. LP provision is passive but regime-dependent: in the 2025 bear market, impermanent loss overwhelmed fee income in SOL/USDC pairs.

These paths are not ruled out as supplementary income sources on existing holdings. They are ruled out as primary paths because they do not leverage what is actually differentiated.

### 3. Monitoring / Alerting / Analytics Product (Path 4) as a standalone primary path

The monitoring product ideas are real (unlock risk monitor, protocol health monitor, funding rate regime tracker). The analytical design capability exists. But building a production-quality monitoring system and then distributing it to paying customers is a 6–12 month engineering and marketing project with highly uncertain revenue. The crypto data product market is crowded with free alternatives funded by protocol grants. Charging $50–$200/month for a monitoring service requires either genuinely unique data or a distribution channel that reaches paying customers — neither of which exists today.

This path is not a fantasy in the abstract. It is a bad primary path given the current constraint set. It works as a complement to Path 2 (the monitoring product generates leads for the research business), not as a standalone income source.
